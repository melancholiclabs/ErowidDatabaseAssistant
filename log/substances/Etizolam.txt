federalLawText :: Etizolam is not controlled in the US. Although it is chemically similar to other benzodiazepines, the U.S. Controlled Substance Analogue Act does not apply, as no similar drugs are in Schedule I or II. Sales of this chemical to end users would likely be considered drug sales and thus regulated by the FDA.
stateLaw :: Alabama	Etizolam became Schedule I in March 2014. See https://legiscan.com/AL/text/SB333/2014. (thanks MA) (last updated Mar 31, 2015)
Arkansas	Etizolam becomes Schedule I in Arkansas on December 1st, 2014. On June 17th, 2014 Arkansas proposed an amendment to the state CSA that would schedule Etizolam as Schedule I, effective December 1st, 2014. On September 2nd, 2014 the Arkansas Board of Health met with public officials and concerned citizens to hear public commenting. Public commenting was in favor of scheduling Etizolam, as well as two other proposed changes. See http://www.healthy.arkansas.gov/aboutADH/RulesRegs/controlled_substances_list.pdf and http://www.healthy.arkansas.gov/aboutADH/Pages/RulesRegulationsProposed.aspx. (last updated Nov 9 2014)
Georgia	Added to Schedule IV in May 2015. See http://www.legis.ga.gov/Legislation/en-US/display/20152016/HB/211. (thanks ch) (last updated Feb 26 2016)
Mississippi	Etizolam was controlled and added to Schedule I in Mississippi by law in April, 2014. See http://legiscan.com/MS/bill/HB1231/2014. (thanks d) (last updated Oct 13, 2014)
