federalLawText :: Chamomile is uncontrolled in the United States. This means all parts of the plant and its extracts are legal to cultivate, buy, possess, and distribute (sell, trade or give) without a license or prescription. If sold as a supplement, sales must conform to U.S. supplement laws. If sold for consumption as a food or drug, sales are regulated by the FDA.	
Anthemis nobilis and Matricaria chamomilla flowers are approved for food use as a spice or seasoning in the U.S. and appear in the FDA's list of substances Generally Recognized As Safe (GRAS).
