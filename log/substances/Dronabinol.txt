description :: Dronabinol is a synthetic form of delta-9-THC, the primary active ingredient in Cannabis. It is presecribed as an appetite stimulant and for treating neuropathic pain in the United States. Dronabinol is one of the examples used to point out the inconsistency of the U.S. and U.N. controlled substance rules and definitions because THC both falls under the classifications as "no accepted medical use" and "accepted medical use".

federalLawText :: While tetrahydrocannabinols, the active chemicals contained in Cannabis plants, are Schedule I in the United States (making them federally illegal to cultivate, buy, possess, or distribute), an exception has been made for dronabinol (synthetic THC). Dronabinol is Schedule III when "in sesame oil and encapsulated in a soft gelatin capsule in a U.S. Food and Drug Administration approved product". Dronabinol was moved from Schedule II to Schedule III in July 1999.
