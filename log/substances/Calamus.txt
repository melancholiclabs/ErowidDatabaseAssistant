botanicalClassification :: Family :Arum, Genus :Acorus, Species :calamuscommonNames :: Sweet Flag; Sweet Sedge; Sweet Root; Myrtle Grass
uses :: Dried calamus rhizome (root) can be used to treat stomach cramps, gas, gastric ulcers, and lack of appetite.
description :: A perennial herb that grows on the water's edge and in wetlands. It sends up leafy, grasslike stems from its underground stalk. The stems may be up to 5 feet tall. Halfway up the stem, each leaf bears a 2 to 4 inch long fleshy cylindrical flower stalk, consisting of many tiny yellow-brown flowers.

federalLawText :: Acorus calamus is uncontrolled in the United States. This means all parts of the plant and its extracts are legal to cultivate, buy, possess, and distribute (sell, trade or give) without a license or prescription.	
Calamus has been banned as a food additive by the FDA in all forms including its extacts and oils. This means it may not be added to food products sold in the U.S.
